01What are sweepstakes casinos?▾

Sweepstakes casinos work like online casinos, but they operate under sweepstakes law instead of gambling law. That's what makes them legal in most US states. Every site uses two currencies: Gold Coins (GC) for fun play with no cash value, and Sweeps Coins (SC) which can be redeemed for real money. SC is what you're farming.

02What is SC (Sweeps Coins)?▾

SC is the currency that actually matters. It's redeemable for real cash, typically at a 1:1 ratio — 100 SC becomes $100. It's given out for free through daily bonuses, login streaks, and promos, never sold directly (that's the legal loophole that makes the whole model work). A few sites use a different ratio or name it something else, but the function is the same everywhere.

Farming means logging into your sites daily and collecting every free reward available — daily bonuses, claim links, free spins, anything no-cost. Eligibility
3 questions
05Is this legal?▾

Yes. These platforms operate under US sweepstakes law, which is a completely separate legal framework from gambling regulation. The dual-currency setup (GC for fun, SC for prizes) combined with mandatory free entry methods is what keeps the model legal. It's legal in most states, though a handful (like WA, ID, and MI) restrict or block sweepstakes casinos entirely — always check your state before signing up.

06How are sweepstakes casinos different from real money online casinos?▾

The biggest difference is the legal structure. Real money casinos require you to deposit actual cash and are heavily regulated by state gaming commissions, available only in a handful of states. Sweepstakes casinos don't involve depositing real money at all — they use the GC/SC system and run under sweepstakes law, which means they're available in far more states. The games themselves can look identical (same providers, same slots), but the way money flows in and out is fundamentally different.

07Do I need to verify my identity?▾

Yes, every legitimate site requires identity verification before you can redeem. Register with your exact legal name from the start (it has to match your ID later) and get verified as early as possible rather than waiting until you're ready to cash out. This avoids delays right when you actually want your money.

11I played 100 SC on a 97% RTP game and ended up with less than 97 SC. Why?▾

RTP is a statistical average calculated over millions of spins, not a guarantee for any single session. It doesn't mean you'll get exactly 97 SC back for every 100 SC wagered each time you play. There's always variance — some sessions land above the RTP, some below. Over a long period across many sessions, your results trend toward the advertised RTP. Short-term swings up or down are completely normal.

4 questions
12What is a playthrough (wagering) requirement?▾

Before you can cash out SC, you typically need to wager it a set number of times. At most sweepstakes casinos this is just 1x playthrough — meaning 10 SC received requires 10 SC in total bets before it's redeemable. Compare that to traditional online casinos where 20x-40x wagering requirements are standard, and you can see why the sweepstakes model is so much more player-friendly.

13How do I redeem my winnings?▾

Once your SC is past playthrough, you submit a redemption request through the site, usually requiring your verified identity to already be on file. Common payout methods include direct bank transfer, PayPal, and gift cards. Processing time varies by site — some pay out within a day, others take a week or more, so factor that into which sites you prioritize.

14Do I have to pay taxes on this?▾

Yes — redeemed winnings are taxable income in the US, same as any gambling-style winnings. Sites that pay out above certain thresholds may issue a W-2G. Keep your own records of what you've redeemed throughout the year since not every site reports automatically. This isn't tax advice — talk to an accountant about your specific situation, especially once you're earning consistently.
